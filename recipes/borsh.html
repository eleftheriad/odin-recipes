Borsh

Description

Borscht is a sour soup common in Eastern Europe and Northern Asia. Depending on the recipe, borscht may include meat or fish, or be purely vegetarian; it may be served either hot or cold, and it may range from a hearty one-pot meal to a clear broth or a smooth drink. It is often served with smetana or sour cream, hard-boiled eggs or potatoes, but there exists an ample choice of more involved garnishes and side dishes, such as uszka or pampushky, that can be served with the soup.

Its popularity has spread throughout Eastern Europe and – by way of migration away from the Russian Empire – to other continents.

Ingredients

  • Meat (beef) - 400g
  • Potatoes (Depends on the pot I mean) - 5
  • Beets - 3
  • Carrots - 3
  • Cabbage - 1 forks
  • Tomato (large) - 1

Steps

  1. Put meat in stew
  2. When the meat is cooked, add the peeled, and cut on cubes potatoes
  3. After 5 minutes add shredded cabbage. Make sure the lid covers the pot half way.
  4. Heat pan, pour a little vegetable oil. Put grated carrots and beets.
  5. Immediately pour 2 tbsp. vinegar and 1 tbsp. of sugar.
  6. Stir, cover and leave to stew for 15 minutes.
